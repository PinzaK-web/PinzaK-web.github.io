Project Introduction

Project Background

In industrial fields, wall inspection of large storage tanks, bridges, wind turbine towers and other equipment has long relied on manual high-altitude operations, which have high safety risks, low inspection efficiency, and high missed detection rates. Especially in harsh environments such as sand and wind, manual operations become extremely difficult, requiring automated equipment as a replacement.

Responding to industrial automation needs, this project explores the feasibility of Climbing Robot technology through graduation design, providing innovative solutions for high-altitude wall inspection and filling the gap in small and medium-sized automated inspection equipment in this field.

Project Goals

Short-term goal: Complete the development of core functions of the Climbing Robot, achieving reliable adsorption, stable movement, automated Flaw detection, and intelligent navigation to meet basic inspection scenario requirements.

Long-term goal: Replace manual high-altitude operations. Through technical optimization, the robot's inspection efficiency will be improved by more than 50%, the detection accuracy will reach over 95%, and zero safety accidents will be guaranteed.

Core Features

Advanced Climbing Mechanism

  • Adapts to multiple wall types: vertical walls (90°), inclined walls (0-90°), meeting different industrial scenario requirements
  • Extreme environment adaptation: temporary operation without power, stable operation in sandy and windy conditions, precise adsorption on irregular surfaces
  • Structural support: adopts Wei Qiuru's reliable adsorption structure and internal support structure to ensure stable adsorption force in different scenarios
  • Lightweight design with high payload capacity for carrying inspection equipment

Advanced Automation Capabilities

  • Autonomous mission planning: pre-programmed inspection routes with minimal human intervention required
  • Self-calibration: automatic system checks and calibration before and during operations
  • Adaptive control: intelligent adjustment of climbing speed and adsorption force based on surface conditions
  • Remote monitoring: real-time data transmission and status updates to control center
  • Automatic charging: capability to return to charging stations when battery levels are low

Precision Flaw Detection System

  • Multi-sensor integration: combines visual, ultrasonic and magnetic flux leakage detection technologies
  • Defect detection: automatically scans walls (focusing on weld areas), accurately identifies surface/near-surface cracks, corrosion, pores and other defects
  • Data collection: real-time recording of detection data (magnetic flux leakage signals, detection images), bound with position information
  • Defect marking: automatically performs inkjet or marking operations when defects are found, facilitating later manual positioning and review
  • AI-powered analysis: machine learning algorithms for improved defect recognition accuracy over time

Intelligent Obstacle Avoidance

  • 360° environmental perception: advanced sensors for detecting obstacles from all directions
  • Real-time mapping: creates and updates environment maps during operation for better navigation
  • Dynamic path planning: instantly calculates alternative routes when obstacles are detected
  • Collision prevention: maintains safe distance from all obstacles while continuing inspection tasks
  • Obstacle classification: distinguishes between temporary and permanent obstacles for appropriate navigation responses
  • Edge detection: identifies and avoids structural edges and drop-offs during operation
